Recent DDoS and ransom attack trends: What you need to know to protect your network

Cloudflare’s network protects approximately 25 million Internet properties, giving us deep insight into DDoS attack trends around the world. We also saw an increase in emergency onboardings of companies under an active DDoS attack (an increase by 41.8% in 2021 H1 compared to 2020 H2). Recently massive ransomware and ransom DDoS attacks interrupted aspects of critical infrastructure around the world, including one of the largest petroleum pipeline system operators and one of the world’s biggest meat processing companies. In July hundreds of US companies were also hit by a ransomware attack demanding 70 million USD in Bitcoin.

In Q2 2021, Cloudflare observed DDoS attack trends over our network that paint a picture that reflects the overall global cyber threat landscape. Read this report to learn:

  • Which attack vectors are becoming more and less common
  • Which industries and countries have experienced the most and least DDoS attacks
  • How large, high bitrate attacks are trending quarter-over-quarter
  • Patterns in ransom DDoS activity, and how to respond if you receive a ransom demand
